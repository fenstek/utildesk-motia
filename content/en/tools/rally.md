# Rally

Rally is an advanced software solution developed specifically for agile project management in enterprises. The platform helps teams plan, track, and manage complex projects efficiently. With a focus on transparency and collaboration, Rally helps put agile methods such as Scrum or Kanban into practice effectively and increase productivity.

## Who is Rally suitable for?

Rally is aimed primarily at mid-sized and large companies that want to implement agile working methods in their development and management processes. The platform is particularly suitable for:

- Agile teams that want to improve collaboration and transparency
- Project managers coordinating complex projects with multiple teams
- Companies seeking agile scaling at the enterprise level
- Organizations looking for a central solution for planning, tracking, and reporting

The solution is suitable for both IT and software development teams as well as other areas that use agile principles.

## Typical Use Cases

- **Focused rollout:** Rally is a good fit when AI, product, and domain teams want to stop improvising a recurring workflow around agile, project management, enterprise.
- **Operations, not demos:** The tool becomes more valuable when prompts, models, outputs, and review steps are documented well enough to survive beyond a one-off trial.
- **Team handovers:** Rally can make responsibilities clearer, so work does not disappear into chats, spreadsheets, or personal accounts.
- **Quality control:** A short review step is especially useful before outputs are published, automated further, or handed over to customers.

## What really matters in daily use

In day-to-day work, Rally is less about having every edge feature and more about whether the team understands where work starts, who reviews it, and how results move forward. A useful setup defines roles, naming rules, and the most important handover points before adoption.

Rally is strongest when it reduces friction in an existing workflow instead of creating a second place to maintain. Before rolling it out widely, test it with real examples: which task becomes faster, which decision becomes clearer, and which manual check should intentionally remain?

## Main features

- **Agile planning and backlog management:** Manage user stories, epics, and features with prioritization and assignment.
- **Sprint and release planning:** Support for creating and managing sprints, including capacity planning.
- **Progress tracking:** Visualize project status using burndown charts, Kanban boards, and dashboards.
- **Team collaboration:** Communication and feedback features to encourage exchange between team members.
- **Reporting and analysis:** Extensive reports for performance measurement and decision support.
- **Scaling agile processes:** Support for frameworks such as SAFe for enterprise-wide agile transformation.
- **Integration:** Interfaces to common tools such as Jira, GitHub, or CI/CD systems.
- **Resource management:** Management of team capacity and task assignment.
- **User and permission management:** Granular control of access rights and roles.
- **Mobile access:** Use the platform on the go via mobile devices.

## Workflow Fit

Rally fits best into a workflow with a clear input, a traceable work step, and a defined finish line. Small teams can usually keep the process lightweight; larger organizations should also define permissions, approvals, and integrations.

If Rally becomes just another account without ownership, the value fades quickly. Give it a clear place in the existing stack: what enters the tool, what gets decided there, and where the result goes next.

## Privacy & Data

Before adopting Rally, clarify which data will enter the tool and whether model outputs, training data, prompts, and user feedback are involved. The more sensitive the material, the more important permissions, retention rules, export options, and a documented decision on what should stay outside the tool become.

For European teams evaluating Rally, data processing agreements, hosting information, and deletion processes are also worth checking. This is not a substitute for legal advice, but it avoids the common mistake of introducing Rally before the data path is understood.

## Editorial Assessment

Rally is strongest when it is treated as one component in a clearly described workflow, not as a magic shortcut. The real benefit comes from less friction, clearer handovers, and more repeatable execution.

Our recommendation is to start with one concrete use case, write down success criteria, and review after two to four weeks whether Rally genuinely saves time or simply creates another system to maintain. That keeps the decision grounded, even when the feature list is long.
