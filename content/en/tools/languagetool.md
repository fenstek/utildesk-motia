# LanguageTool

LanguageTool is a multilingual writing assistant for spelling, grammar, punctuation, and style. It is particularly useful when German, English, and other languages are written regularly and a first quality check should happen directly in a browser or text editor. The project has open-source roots, while the service and available features differ by use case and plan.

## Who is LanguageTool for?

LanguageTool works well for editorial, marketing, support, and knowledge teams that regularly review short and medium-length copy before publication. It is also useful for individuals writing in more than one language. Its value is highest where a house style and terminology already exist: the tool catches avoidable errors and inconsistencies, while a person retains the final decision.

It is not a substitute for creative editing, legal review, or highly specialised technical review. In those cases it is an additional checkpoint, not the editor of record.

## What matters in daily use

LanguageTool has the most impact when it occupies a defined point in the workflow: before a ticket is created, before a newsletter is sent, or before a knowledge-base article is approved. Without that agreement, suggestions are either accepted blindly or ignored permanently.

Test a typical set of texts with real brand names, specialist terms, and two target languages. Record which suggestions help, which create false changes, and which terms belong in a personal or team dictionary. That is more useful than a theoretical accuracy score.

## Key capabilities

- Spelling, grammar, style, and punctuation checks across many languages and variants.
- Browser extensions and integrations for common writing environments, including office and document workflows.
- Suggestions for clearer phrasing, tone, and word choice.
- Personal dictionaries and adaptable rules for recurring names and terminology.
- Premium checks with additional depth in selected major languages; current limits are published by the provider.
- Self-hostable open-source components for scenarios that can support the required operations and licence review.

## Limits and common mistakes

A grammatically correct sentence can still be factually wrong or unsuitable for the audience. LanguageTool has limited project context and should not replace source verification, legal review, or approval. Style suggestions remain suggestions: a deliberate specialist formulation should not be flattened just to satisfy a checker.

Quality also varies by language. For important text types, run a test in the relevant language variant rather than assuming every rule set is equally deep.

## Privacy and governance

When text is checked through an extension or cloud service, review what content is transmitted, which settings apply, and whether sensitive fields can be excluded. For teams, data-processing terms, administrator control, user management, and a clear policy for customer and personnel data matter more than an arbitrary free installation.

## Pricing and rollout

There is a free base offering and paid options with broader checks and team features. Verify current character limits, supported capabilities, and enterprise terms directly with the provider. A good rollout starts with one small editorial or support team, a maintained dictionary, and one rule: suggestions are reviewed, not accepted automatically.

## Alternatives to LanguageTool

- [Grammarly](/en/tools/grammarly/): when English and tone suggestions are the primary concern.
- [Microsoft Editor](/en/tools/microsoft-editor/): when review should stay closely connected to Microsoft 365 work.
- [ProWritingAid](/en/tools/prowritingaid/): when writers need more detailed style and manuscript analysis.
- [Wordtune](/en/tools/wordtune/): when rewriting and generating variants matter more than rule-based correction.

## Editorial assessment

LanguageTool is a dependable first quality filter, especially for multilingual teams. Its best role is not to make writing automatically correct, but to free reviewers from avoidable errors. With maintained terminology and a human review of claims, tone, and facts, it is genuinely useful. Without that discipline, it merely creates more suggestions that nobody owns.

## FAQ

**Does LanguageTool support German and English well?**

Both are important supported languages. The scope and depth of particular checks can vary by plan and language variant.

**Can LanguageTool check sensitive text?**

That depends on the use mode, settings, and contract. Teams should review data flow and privacy before using customer or personnel material.

**Does LanguageTool replace an editor?**

No. It helps with formal and stylistic signals, but it does not verify facts, legal correctness, or subject expertise.
