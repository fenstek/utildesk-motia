# Greenhouse

Greenhouse is a cloud recruiting platform that brings applications, interviews, hiring decisions and the handoff into onboarding into one operating process. Its value is not an automatically better selection; it is clearer accountability: who reviews an application, which criteria guide an interview, when feedback becomes visible and who makes the decision. That framework is useful for organizations hiring regularly with several participants. For occasional single hires, implementation and maintenance can outweigh the benefit of a dedicated system.

## Who is Greenhouse for?

Greenhouse is aimed primarily at recruiting and HR teams coordinating multiple openings, hiring managers and interviewers. The vendor positions the platform across sourcing, applications, interviewing, offers and onboarding. That breadth fits organizations seeking a consistent process across departments or regions. A small company making a few straightforward hires should first compare whether a lighter ATS can provide enough ownership, traceability and candidate communication with less administration.

## What belongs in the workflow?

The ATS centralizes jobs, candidate profiles, applications and pipeline stages. Interview plans and interview kits give interviewers role context, preparation, questions and a defined evaluation framework. Scorecards capture previously agreed skills, traits or qualifications and an overall recommendation; they do not replace professional judgment or accountable decision-making. Depending on the purchased scope, the platform also covers job publishing, career pages, candidate communication, talent pools, reporting, offers and onboarding activities.

## A workable hiring workflow

Before opening a job, the team should define stages, owners and decision criteria. For a backend role, that might mean recruiter review, a technical conversation, a systems-design interview and a final manager discussion. Each stage gets an interview kit with a small set of relevant focus attributes. After the conversation, every interviewer submits a scorecard promptly and independently; the hiring group then compares the reasoning. Rejection reasons and stage rules should be explicit enough to prevent candidates from sitting in an undefined status for weeks.

## Implementation, integrations and operations

Start with one representative job instead of modeling every exception at once. The rollout needs decisions about roles and permissions, calendars, email, career pages, job boards, background checks and the handoff to an HRIS or onboarding system. Greenhouse documents pre-built integrations and several APIs. A custom connection still needs authentication controls, data minimization, failure handling, monitoring and a clean shutdown path if the vendor or workflow changes. A monthly process review can expose duplicated stages, overdue candidates, missing scorecards and excessive access.

## Quality and decision controls

A completed scorecard does not prove that a selection process is fair or predictive. Teams should check whether criteria match the role, whether interviewers interpret them consistently, how long candidates wait at each stage and how often recruiters repair the workflow manually. Scorecard samples, source and funnel reports, and stage-time reviews create useful evidence. AI-assisted summaries or matching features should support review, not trigger an unexamined rejection. The employer remains responsible for the decision and for documenting exceptions.

## Privacy, security and governance

Applications commonly contain resumes, contact details, interview notes and sometimes sensitive personal information. Before importing data, define retention periods, deletion workflows, role boundaries, private notes and access for external recruiters. Greenhouse publishes information about its DPA, subprocessors, encryption, SSO, SCIM, audit-log API and compliance program. Those materials support due diligence but do not constitute an automatic approval for a particular jurisdiction: the customer must assess purpose, lawful basis, transfers, candidate rights, vendor access and exit procedures for its own hiring process.

## Pricing and total cost

Greenhouse does not publish a simple universal price list. The vendor describes Core, Plus and Pro plans, with the quote shaped by hiring needs, organizational complexity and required capabilities. The business case should include configuration of jobs and scorecards, migration, training, integration maintenance, support, reporting and recurring privacy and permission reviews. Before signing, run a real process through the proposed plan and confirm which governance and integration features are included rather than treating the demo scope as the contracted scope.

## Editorial assessment

Greenhouse is recommended for recruiting organizations with recurring hiring, several interviewers and a genuine commitment to structured decisions. It creates value when the company agrees on criteria, ownership and retention rules before configuration, then reviews process data regularly. A smaller ATS is preferable for a small team with few uncomplicated hires or no capacity for data stewardship. A useful pilot is one complete job from application to decision, measured by stage waiting time, scorecard completion, manual rework and whether the final rationale is understandable to an independent reviewer.

## Alternatives

- [Lever](/en/tools/lever/): An ATS and recruiting CRM with a stronger emphasis on talent pipelines and relationship work when sourcing and nurturing are central.
- [Workable](/en/tools/workable/): A more approachable recruiting platform for teams that need to publish jobs quickly without as much process complexity.
- [SmartRecruiters](/en/tools/smartrecruiters/): A broader talent-acquisition approach for organizations needing international hiring and a large integration ecosystem.
- [BambooHR](/en/tools/bamboohr/): An HR suite with recruiting capabilities when applicant tracking should connect closely to employee records and HR administration.

## FAQ

**Is Greenhouse suitable for a small business?**

It depends on hiring volume and coordination needs. With only a few open roles, compare setup, administration and governance costs with a lighter ATS. Repeated hiring and many interview participants make Greenhouse easier to justify.

**What is the difference between an interview kit and a scorecard?**

An interview kit provides preparation, questions and role context for a conversation. A scorecard records the resulting evaluation against defined criteria. Both require job-specific criteria and prompt feedback to be useful.

**Can Greenhouse connect to other HR systems?**

Yes. Greenhouse documents pre-built integrations and APIs for areas including applications, jobs, assessments, onboarding and sourcing. Test permissions, fields, failure modes and connection shutdown before moving a critical workflow into production.

**How is Greenhouse priced?**

The vendor describes Core, Plus and Pro and provides a customized quote. Hiring needs, organizational complexity and required capabilities affect the price. Migration, integration work, training and ongoing governance are additional internal costs.

**Does Greenhouse make the hiring decision for the team?**

No. It structures information and workflow. Scorecards, reports and AI-assisted features may support review, but they should not be treated as unsupervised selection decisions. The organization must retain criteria, exception handling and final accountability.
