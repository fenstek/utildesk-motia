# Valohai

Valohai is an MLOps platform specifically designed to automate and manage the entire lifecycle of machine learning projects. The platform helps data scientists and developers train, version, and scale models efficiently, both in the cloud and on-premises. With Valohai, companies can deliver their AI projects faster and more reliably by ensuring repeatable workflows and centralized experiment management.

## Who is Valohai suitable for?

Valohai is aimed primarily at companies and teams that want to integrate machine learning models into production-ready applications and place value on automation, scalability, and reproducibility. In particular, data scientists, ML engineers, and developers who build and manage complex training pipelines benefit from the platform’s features. Organizations that want to run their AI projects in the cloud or in hybrid environments will also find Valohai to be a flexible solution. For beginners in machine learning, the platform may be somewhat complex because of its technical depth.

## Key features

- **End-to-end MLOps platform:** Management of the complete machine learning lifecycle, from data preparation through model training to deployment.
- **Automated pipelines:** Creation and automation of repeatable training and evaluation processes.
- **Version control:** Traceability of models, datasets, and training code through integrated versioning.
- **Cloud and on-premises support:** Flexible deployment and scaling across different infrastructures.
- **Experiment tracking:** Detailed documentation and comparison of training runs and model results.
- **Developer tool integration:** Compatibility with common tools such as Git, Docker, and Kubernetes.
- **Scalability:** Support for distributed training and use of GPU and TPU resources.
- **Security and compliance:** Management of access rights and adherence to company policies.
- **API access:** Programmatic control and automation through a comprehensive API.
- **User-friendly dashboard:** Clear visualization of projects, pipelines, and resources.

## Typical Use Cases

- **Focused rollout:** Valohai is a good fit when AI, product, and domain teams want to stop improvising a recurring workflow around MLOps, machine learning, cloud.
- **Operations, not demos:** The tool becomes more valuable when prompts, models, outputs, and review steps are documented well enough to survive beyond a one-off trial.
- **Team handovers:** Valohai can make responsibilities clearer, so work does not disappear into chats, spreadsheets, or personal accounts.
- **Quality control:** A short review step is especially useful before outputs are published, automated further, or handed over to customers.

## What really matters in daily use

In day-to-day work, Valohai is less about having every edge feature and more about whether the team understands where work starts, who reviews it, and how results move forward. A useful setup defines roles, naming rules, and the most important handover points before adoption.

Valohai is strongest when it reduces friction in an existing workflow instead of creating a second place to maintain. Before rolling it out widely, test it with real examples: which task becomes faster, which decision becomes clearer, and which manual check should intentionally remain?

## Workflow Fit

Valohai fits best into a workflow with a clear input, a traceable work step, and a defined finish line. Small teams can usually keep the process lightweight; larger organizations should also define permissions, approvals, and integrations.

If Valohai becomes just another account without ownership, the value fades quickly. Give it a clear place in the existing stack: what enters the tool, what gets decided there, and where the result goes next.

## Privacy & Data

Before adopting Valohai, clarify which data will enter the tool and whether model outputs, training data, prompts, and user feedback are involved. The more sensitive the material, the more important permissions, retention rules, export options, and a documented decision on what should stay outside the tool become.

For European teams evaluating Valohai, data processing agreements, hosting information, and deletion processes are also worth checking. This is not a substitute for legal advice, but it avoids the common mistake of introducing Valohai before the data path is understood.

## Editorial Assessment

Valohai is strongest when it is treated as one component in a clearly described workflow, not as a magic shortcut. The real benefit comes from less friction, clearer handovers, and more repeatable execution.

Our recommendation is to start with one concrete use case, write down success criteria, and review after two to four weeks whether Valohai genuinely saves time or simply creates another system to maintain. That keeps the decision grounded, even when the feature list is long.
