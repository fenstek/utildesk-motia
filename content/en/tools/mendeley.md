# Mendeley

Mendeley is a powerful reference manager and academic social network that helps researchers organize literature, manage citations, and create scholarly papers efficiently. The software supports users with research, collaboration, and source management to make the academic workflow easier.

## Who is Mendeley for?

Mendeley is aimed primarily at students, doctoral candidates, researchers, and academic professionals who regularly work with scholarly publications. It is suitable for anyone who needs extensive literature management to systematically organize sources and insert citations correctly in academic papers. Teams working together on research projects also benefit from the collaboration features.

## Key Features

- **Reference management:** Easily organize PDFs and other documents in personal libraries with tags and notes.
- **Automatic metadata detection:** Imports bibliographic data and automatically fills in missing information.
- **Citation plugin:** Integrates with word processors (e.g. Microsoft Word, LibreOffice) for easy insertion of citations and creation of bibliographies.
- **Cloud synchronization:** Syncs the library across different devices.
- **Collaboration:** Shared folders and groups for working with other researchers.
- **Recommendations:** Suggestions for relevant literature based on your own library.
- **PDF reader with annotations:** Mark, comment on, and highlight text directly in the PDF.
- **Social network:** Connect with other researchers and share research results.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- Intuitive user interface that makes getting started easy.
- Extensive reference management and citation features.
- Support for many citation styles.
- Cloud-based synchronization enables access from anywhere.
- Free basic version with many features.
- Promotes academic collaboration through group features.

### Cons

- Limited storage space in the free version.
- Occasional issues with automatic metadata detection.
- The user interface can feel a bit sluggish on some systems.
- Some advanced features are only available in paid plans.
- Privacy concerns with cloud storage of sensitive data depending on user requirements.

## Pricing & Costs

Mendeley offers a freemium model. The basic version is free and already includes many important features for individuals. For more storage, advanced group features, and additional features, there are paid subscriptions whose prices may vary depending on the provider and plan.

## Alternatives to Mendeley

- **Zotero:** Open-source reference manager with extensive features and strong community support.
- **EndNote:** Professional reference management program with extensive citation options, often used in academic institutions.
- **Citavi:** Combination of reference management and knowledge organization, especially popular in German-speaking regions.
- **RefWorks:** Cloud-based solution for reference management and collaboration, often offered by libraries.
- **Paperpile:** Modern web-based solution, especially suitable for Google Drive users.

## FAQ

**How does automatic metadata detection work in Mendeley?**  
Mendeley analyzes uploaded PDFs and tries to extract bibliographic data such as author, title, and year of publication. Accuracy depends on the quality of the documents.

**Can I use Mendeley offline?**  
Yes, the Mendeley desktop application works without an internet connection. Changes are synchronized the next time you go online.

**Which word processors are supported?**  
Mendeley offers plugins for Microsoft Word and LibreOffice to insert citations directly into documents.

**How secure is my data in Mendeley?**  
Mendeley stores data in the cloud, with privacy policies varying by region. Users should protect sensitive data accordingly and review the privacy terms.

**Is there a mobile app for Mendeley?**  
Yes, Mendeley offers mobile apps for iOS and Android, allowing users to manage their library on the go.

**Can I collaborate with teams in Mendeley?**  
Yes, through group features, users can share documents and work together on reference management.

**Is Mendeley suitable for all subject areas?**  
Mendeley can be used across disciplines and is especially popular in the natural sciences, social sciences, and humanities.

**How can I import my library from other programs?**  
Mendeley supports importing data from various formats such as BibTeX, RIS, and EndNote XML to make switching easier.
