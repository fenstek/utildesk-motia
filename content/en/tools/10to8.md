# 10to8

10to8 is a scheduling and booking system for teams that no longer want to coordinate external appointments through long email threads. The important question is not whether a booking link exists, but whether availability rules, reminders, ownership and cancellations become easier to manage in daily work.

## Who is 10to8 for?

- Consultants, clinics, educators and service teams with recurring customer appointments.
- Teams that need to coordinate several calendars, rooms or staff members in one booking process.
- Organizations that want to reduce no-shows without chasing every appointment manually.

## Typical use cases

- public booking pages for calls, office hours, sessions or training appointments
- automatic confirmations and reminders for customers or participants
- team availability with buffers, time zones and internal ownership
- basic analysis of cancellations, booking bottlenecks and manual coordination work

## What really matters in daily use

In daily use, 10to8 depends on the quality of the rules behind the booking page. Slot length, buffers, cancellation windows, responsible people and exceptions should be defined before the link is shared. Otherwise the tool looks convenient at first and then creates follow-up questions after each booking.

## Workflow Fit

10to8 fits best when the appointment type is repeatable and does not require heavy pre-qualification every time. For complex advisory work, it should be paired with intake forms, CRM notes or an internal handoff step.

## Limits and control points

Before 10to8 is rolled out more broadly, the team should write down three things: which task the booking rules and no-show rate actually improves, who owns maintenance and how a bad run will be recognized. Useful control points are a before-and-after comparison, a clear escalation path and a short review after the first real cases.

Without these points, 10to8 can look like progress while creating new maintenance work. The pilot succeeds when decisions become more visible, not when another channel, report or integration point simply appears.

## Privacy and data notes

Scheduling can involve names, email addresses, phone numbers, calendar data and short descriptions of a request. Teams should decide which fields are really necessary, how long booking data is retained and whether sensitive health, HR or contract details should stay out of the form.

## Pricing and costs

The entry cost can be attractive for small teams, but real cost depends on SMS reminders, team seats, integrations and ongoing maintenance of booking rules. A pilot should measure saved coordination time, not just license price.

## Alternatives to 10to8

- [Acuity Scheduling](/en/tools/acuity-scheduling/): stronger for forms, payments and service-oriented booking pages.
- [Calendly](/en/tools/calendly/): a faster entry point for simple scheduling links and calendar rules.
- [Setmore](/en/tools/setmore/): practical for small teams with staff, services and customer bookings.
- [SimplyBook.me](/en/tools/simplybook-me/): more industry add-ons for service businesses with richer booking flows.
- [Bookafy](/en/tools/bookafy/): an alternative for flexible time slots, team calendars and payment integration.

## Editorial Assessment

10to8 is useful when a team handles many similar appointments and needs a reliable external booking layer. It is weaker when the real bottleneck is preparation, prioritization or follow-up rather than the booking itself.

## FAQ

### What is a good first test for 10to8?

A useful test takes one real, bounded process and checks afterwards whether there are fewer follow-up questions, fewer manual corrections and clearer handoffs. For 10to8, the test should resemble daily work rather than a polished demo.

### When is 10to8 a poor fit?

10to8 is a poor fit when ownership, data quality or approvals are still unclear. In that situation the tool often amplifies existing process problems instead of solving them.

### Which alternative should be compared first?

That depends on the bottleneck. If the bottleneck is simpler, cheaper or more specialized, compare Acuity Scheduling or Calendly first.

### What should teams define before rollout?

Before rollout, teams should define owners, data sources, approvals, error cases and success criteria. That keeps 10to8 inside a controlled workflow instead of turning it into another maintenance task.

### Can 10to8 reduce no-shows?

It can reduce no-shows when reminders, cancellation rules and clear booking instructions are configured well. It does not replace proper expectation setting before the appointment.
