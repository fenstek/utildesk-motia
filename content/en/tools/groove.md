# Groove

Groove is an AI-powered helpdesk software that helps companies make their customer service more efficient and productive. With automated workflows, intelligent reply suggestions, and a user-friendly interface, Groove simplifies the handling of support requests and improves customer satisfaction.

## Who is Groove suitable for?

Groove is primarily aimed at small to medium-sized businesses that want to build centralized and intelligent customer service. The tool is especially useful for support teams that receive many inquiries through different channels (email, chat, social media) and want to save time. Startups and service providers that want to increase productivity and answer customer inquiries more quickly also benefit from Groove.

## Main features

- **Multichannel support:** Manage customer inquiries from email, chat, social media, and other channels in one place.
- **Automated workflows:** Automatic assignment and prioritization of tickets to optimize support processes.
- **AI-based reply suggestions:** Intelligent text suggestions for faster and more consistent responses to customer inquiries.
- **Team collaboration:** Joint ticket handling with internal notes and comments.
- **Reports and analytics:** Evaluation of support statistics to improve service quality.
- **Integration with other tools:** Connection to CRM systems, project management software, and other applications.
- **Self-service portal:** Allows customers to find answers to common questions on their own.
- **Mobile app:** Manage support on the go and answer customer inquiries at any time.

Groove offers a subscription model with various plans based on team size and the desired features. Details on pricing and features vary by provider and plan.

## What really matters in daily use

Groove is interesting as a support and customer communication tool when a team needs simple inbox structures, knowledge articles, and customer history without overloaded enterprise complexity. The practical test is whether requests are answered faster and responsibilities become clearer.

## Workflow Fit

- Good for small support teams, SaaS startups, and service teams moving from a shared inbox to a structured helpdesk.
- Less suitable for very large organizations with complex SLAs, workforce management, and deep call-center processes.

## Editorial Assessment

Groove wins through focus. It is strong when a team wants to professionalize support without immediately adopting a heavy system.
