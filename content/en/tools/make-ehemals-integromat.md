# Make (formerly Integromat)

Make, formerly known as Integromat, is a powerful automation platform that makes it possible to connect different applications and services and create complex workflows. With a visual editor, users can automate processes, synchronize data, and simplify repetitive tasks without any programming knowledge. Make offers a wide range of integrations and supports both simple and complex automation scenarios.

## Who is Make (formerly Integromat) suitable for?

Make is aimed at individuals, small and medium-sized businesses, and teams that want to make their workflows more efficient. The tool is especially suitable for:

- Marketing and sales teams that want to combine data from different sources.
- Developers and IT professionals implementing complex integrations and automations.
- Freelancers and self-employed professionals who want to automate repetitive tasks.
- Companies that want to connect different cloud services without much technical effort.

The platform is ideal for users looking for flexible and scalable automations while placing value on a visual, intuitive interface.

## Key features

- **Visual workflow editor:** Drag-and-drop interface for creating and customizing automations.
- **Broad integration:** Support for hundreds of apps and services such as Google Workspace, Slack, Shopify, Airtable, and more.
- **Automated data processing:** Extract, transform, and load (ETL) data between different sources.
- **Error handling & retries:** Automatic detection and resolution of errors in workflows.
- **Time- and event-based automations:** Workflows can be triggered on a schedule or by specific events.
- **API and HTTP modules:** Enable custom connections to services without prebuilt integrations.
- **Team collaboration:** Shared use and editing of scenarios within a team.
- **Monitoring and logging:** Real-time monitoring of workflows and detailed reports.
- **Freemium model:** Get started with a free plan and upgrade options for more capacity and features.

## Pros and cons

### Pros
- Intuitive visual way to build automations without programming knowledge.
- Extensive app integrations for a wide range of use cases.
- Flexible freemium model with a good entry point.
- Powerful error handling and retry mechanisms.
- Scalable from simple to complex workflows.
- Good documentation and community support.

### Cons
- Complex scenarios may require some time to learn.
- Costs for higher plans can rise quickly depending on usage.
- Some integrations require technical understanding.
- Limited offline functionality because it is cloud-based.

## Pricing & costs

Make offers a freemium model that provides a good starting point with basic features. The free plans often include a limited number of operations and scenarios per month. For larger teams or more extensive automations, there are various paid subscriptions that, depending on the provider and plan, offer different numbers of operations, faster execution, and expanded support. Prices and included features may vary, so it is recommended to check the current terms directly on the provider's website.

## Alternatives to Make (formerly Integromat)

- **Zapier:** Also a leading tool for workflow automation, focused on ease of use and a large number of integrations.
- **Microsoft Power Automate:** Especially suitable for users in the Microsoft ecosystem with extensive automation options.
- **Automate.io:** Offers similar features with a focus on marketing and sales channels.
- **n8n:** Open-source alternative with flexible customization options and self-hosting.
- **Workato:** Enterprise-oriented platform with advanced integration and automation features.

## FAQ

**1. Do I need programming knowledge to use Make?**  
No, Make offers a visual interface that also allows users without programming knowledge to create automations. For more complex scenarios, technical understanding can be helpful.

**2. Which apps can I connect with Make?**  
Make supports hundreds of apps and services, including popular tools such as Google Workspace, Slack, Shopify, Airtable, Dropbox, and many more. The list is continuously growing.

**3. How does the freemium model work?**  
The free plan usually includes a limited number of operations and scenarios. Paid plans can be booked for more capacity and additional features.

**4. Is Make secure for business data?**  
Make uses encrypted connections and follows industry-standard security practices. However, when handling sensitive data, you should still review the respective privacy policies.

**5. Can I connect Make to my own APIs?**  
Yes, custom connections to services that are not supported by default can be created via HTTP modules and API integrations.

**6. Is there a way to monitor workflows?**  
Make provides monitoring and logging features that let you track the execution of your automations in real time.

**7. How quickly are automations executed?**  
Execution speed depends on the chosen plan and the complexity of the workflows. Higher plans often offer faster intervals and more parallel executions.

**8. Can I use Make as a team?**  
Yes, Make supports team features, allowing multiple users to work on automations together and share scenarios.
