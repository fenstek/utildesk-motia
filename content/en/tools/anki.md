# Anki

Anki is a powerful digital learning platform that relies on the principle of repeated questioning. With flashcards, Anki supports long-term retention of knowledge through the so-called Spaced Repetition System (SRS). The software is particularly suitable for students, students, and anyone who wants to learn effectively and sustainably.

## For whom is Anki suitable?

Anki is suitable for learners of all age groups who want to deepen their knowledge systemically. It is particularly suitable for:

- Students who need to learn large amounts of specialized knowledge, vocabulary, or facts.
- Language learners who want to expand their vocabulary efficiently.
- Working professionals who need to prepare for certifications or exams.
- Teachers and trainers who want to create didactically prepared learning content.
- Anyone who wants to create personalized learning content and have access to it at any time.

The open structure of Anki allows for individual adaptation of learning content and adaptation to various subject areas.

## Main Functions

- **Spaced Repetition System (SRS):** Automated repetition of learning content at the optimal time to promote long-term memory.
- **Individual Card Creation:** Text, images, audio, and even videos can be integrated into cards.
- **Multi-Platform Support:** Available for Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS.
- **Synchronization:** Synchronization of learning progress across multiple devices.
- **Large Community and Shared Decks:** Access to a wide range of pre-made learning sets on various topics.
- **Adjustable Learning Intervals:** Users can set individual learning intervals and difficulty levels.
- **Statistics and Learning Progress Analysis:** Overview of the learning process and the efficiency of learning.
- **Add-ons and Extensions:** Possibility to extend functionality with plugins.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Effective learning through scientifically-based repetition system.
- High flexibility in shaping learning content.
- Large and active user community with many free resources.
- Platform-agnostic availability and synchronization.
- Open-source character of the desktop client (free to use).
- Supports various media formats in learning cards.

### Disadvantages

- The user interface can be complex for beginners.
- Mobile apps (especially iOS) are sometimes paid.
- Learning content must be created or searched most of the time - no classic course offering.
- For optimal use, a certain discipline in learning is required.

## Prices & Costs

Anki Desktop is available as free and open-source software. The Android app is usually free, while the iOS app requires a one-time payment. There are no subscriptions, but a Freemium model with optional paid apps. Additional features and add-ons are usually free or provided by the community.

## Alternatives to Anki

- **Quizlet:** A user-friendly flashcard app with many pre-made learning sets and collaborative features. Freemium model.
- **Memrise:** Language learning platform with gamified learning cards and videos. Freemium model.
- **Brainscape:** Flashcard app with adaptive learning algorithm. Freemium with paid upgrades.
- **SuperMemo:** Pioneer of spaced repetition learning with extensive features. Paid.
- **Tinycards (formerly by Duolingo):** Simple to use flashcard app, now discontinued, but similar apps exist.

## FAQ

**1. Is Anki suitable for beginners?**  
Yes, Anki can be used by beginners, but requires an adaptation to the interface and the principle of learning cards.

**2. On which devices can I use Anki?**  
Anki is available for Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS. The synchronization allows learning on multiple devices.

**3. Does Anki cost money?**  
The desktop version is free and open-source. The Android app is usually free, while the iOS app requires a one-time payment.

**4. How does the Spaced Repetition System work?**  
The system adjusts the repetition intervals automatically based on the individual learning progress to minimize forgetting.

**5. Can I use pre-made learning sets?**  
Yes, there is a large community that provides free pre-made learning cards.

**6. How do I create my own learning cards?**  
Anki offers an intuitive interface for creating cards with text, images, audio, and more.

**7. Do I need the internet to use Anki?**  
The desktop and mobile apps can be used offline. For synchronization, an internet connection is required.

**8. Is there support or help with problems?**  
There are extensive online documentation, forums, and an active community that provides support.
