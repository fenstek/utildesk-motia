---# Salesforce Lightning

Salesforce Lightning refers to Salesforce's modern platform interface and development base for CRM-centered apps, automation, and workflows. In the current AI context it matters because the data model, processes, and Agentforce or Einstein capabilities live on the same platform.

## Who Is It For?

Lightning fits Salesforce teams, CRM admins, low-code builders, developers, and companies with strong sales, service, or partner processes. It is most valuable when Salesforce is already the central customer system.

## Typical Use Cases

- Build CRM-adjacent apps and interfaces.
- Connect sales and service processes with automation.
- Align internal workflows with Salesforce data.
- Embed AI and agent functions into existing customer-data processes.

## What Matters In Daily Work

Daily value depends on the quality of the Salesforce data model. Weak fields, duplicate records, and unclear permissions are not solved by Lightning components; they become more visible.

## Key Features

- Component-based Salesforce interfaces.
- Low-code and developer options.
- Automation through Salesforce platform functions.
- Integration with CRM data, roles, and permissions.

## Strengths And Limits

### Strengths

- Strong when Salesforce is already the core system.
- Connects UI, data, and workflow close to CRM.
- Good for standardized enterprise processes.

### Limits

- Tightly bound to Salesforce architecture and licensing.
- Customization can become complex over time.
- Good governance matters more than many new components.

## Workflow Fit

Salesforce Lightning should not be introduced as an isolated tool. The better starting point is a bounded workflow with input data, owners, a review step, and a decision about where results move next. For this card, the most natural first test is to build CRM-adjacent apps and interfaces.

## Privacy And Data

Salesforce data often contains customer, contract, and revenue information. Roles, field permissions, external apps, and AI access should be reviewed strictly.

## Pricing And Costs

Salesforce Lightning is listed as Subscription. Costs depend on Salesforce products, editions, user roles, add-ons, and implementation effort.

## Editorial Assessment

Salesforce Lightning makes sense when CRM processes should not be rebuilt outside the customer system. For teams without Salesforce at the core, it is rarely the leanest starting point.

## FAQ

**What is Salesforce Lightning mainly used for?**

Salesforce Lightning is mainly used for build crm-adjacent apps and interfaces. The tool should be judged by the concrete workflow rather than by the brand name alone.

**Is Salesforce Lightning suitable for teams?**

For Salesforce Lightning: yes, if ownership, access, and review rules are clear. The team should define who maintains the setup and how results are checked.

**What should be tested before rollout?**

Before rolling out Salesforce Lightning, test real data, permissions, costs, export options, and failure cases. A polished demo is not enough for a durable decision.

**When is Salesforce Lightning a poor fit?**

Salesforce Lightning is a poor fit when the team has no clear process, no data rules, or no owner for maintenance after the first setup.
