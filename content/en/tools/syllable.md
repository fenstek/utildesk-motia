# Syllable

Syllable is an agentic AI platform for building and operating AI agents across voice/phone, SMS, chat, and email. The platform is aimed primarily at contact centers and companies that want to deploy fully automated, multilingual customer service agents without having to build complex infrastructure. Syllable offers both a no-code interface (Syllable Console) and SDKs for TypeScript and Python.

## Who is Syllable suitable for?

Syllable is designed for contact center teams, IT departments, and developers in medium-sized and large companies that want to introduce AI-powered customer service automation. Typical use cases include customer support, HR processes, financial inquiries, and general request handling across multiple channels. Organizations that need to handle high call or message volumes and want to rely on human-like, multilingual agents benefit especially from it.

## Key features

- **Multi-channel agents:** Use across phone (voice), SMS, web chat, and email from a central platform
- **No-code console:** Visual interface for creating, testing, and deploying AI agents without programming knowledge
- **Developer SDK:** TypeScript and Python SDKs for deep integration into existing systems
- **Multilingual support:** Support for English, Spanish, Portuguese, French, Cantonese, Mandarin, Vietnamese, Korean, and Russian
- **Auto-scaling:** Automatic scaling during traffic spikes without manual configuration
- **Monitoring & analytics:** Real-time tracking of voice accuracy, response times, conversation success, and error logs
- **Microsoft Azure integration:** Native integration through the Microsoft Marketplace

## Pros and cons

### Pros

- Complete multi-channel coverage (voice, SMS, chat, email) in one platform
- Access through both a no-code interface and developer SDKs
- Built-in auto-scaling reduces infrastructure overhead
- Broad multilingual support for international contact centers
- Available through Microsoft Azure Marketplace

### Cons

- No free entry-level plan (enterprise pricing model)
- Prices are not publicly visible, so custom quotes are required
- Primarily built for medium-sized and large companies; not ideal for individuals
- Strong dependence on cloud infrastructure

## Pricing & costs

Syllable positions itself as an enterprise solution with custom pricing. No public pricing information is available on the website. Interested companies must contact the team directly to receive a quote. A free trial or freemium offering is not known.

## FAQ

**What sets Syllable apart from simple chatbot solutions?**
Syllable is designed for agentic, multilingual AI agents that can operate across multiple channels at the same time, including telephony (voice). Simple chatbots usually cover text chat only.

**Which languages does Syllable support?**
The platform supports English, Spanish, Portuguese, French, Cantonese, Mandarin, Vietnamese, Korean, and Russian.

**Do I need programming knowledge to use Syllable?**
No. The Syllable Console is a no-code interface. For deeper integrations, an SDK in TypeScript and Python is also available.

**Is Syllable suitable for small businesses?**
Syllable is primarily aimed at contact centers and enterprise customers. There is currently no freemium offering for small teams.

**How does Syllable integrate with existing systems?**
Through the SDK (TypeScript/Python) and native Azure integration, Syllable can be connected to CRM systems, databases, and other business applications.

**How does Syllable scale with high request volume?**
The platform scales automatically (auto-scaling), making it suitable even for short-term traffic spikes without manual intervention.
