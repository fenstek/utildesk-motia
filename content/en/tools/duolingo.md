# Duolingo

Duolingo is a mobile and web learning platform built around short, repeatable language lessons. Its courses combine reading, writing, listening, and speaking exercises with review, stories, and game-like motivation. That makes Duolingo useful as an accessible starting point or daily maintenance routine—not automatically a complete language program with a teacher, specialist vocabulary, or verified professional fluency.

## What Duolingo covers

Courses are organized into units and short lessons. Depending on the language and course stage, learners encounter vocabulary, grammar patterns, translation, dictation, listening, and speaking tasks. Duolingo also offers products such as Math, Music, and Chess; those should not be treated as interchangeable with the language curriculum. Content availability depends on the language, platform, and ongoing course changes.

## Who should use it?

Duolingo fits individuals, families, and education settings that need a low-friction way to practise regularly. A company can use it as an optional supplement for everyday vocabulary or as preparation for a longer course. Anyone who needs a fixed grammar progression, exam preparation, or job-specific language should first test whether the relevant course actually covers that goal.

## A practical learning workflow

1. Define the target language, starting level, and a concrete outcome; use placement options when appropriate.
2. Complete a short section on three to five days each week, keeping reminders useful rather than distracting.
3. Use the Practice tab to revisit mistakes, words, listening, or speaking instead of optimising only for points.
4. Run a weekly external check: write a message, understand an audio instruction, or role-play a real situation.

Streaks, XP, and leaderboards are motivation signals, not proof that a learner can communicate freely. A school or onboarding programme therefore needs a separate comprehension and transfer criterion owned by a teacher or programme lead.

## Features and everyday limits

The core courses combine reading, writing, listening, and speaking with personalised review. Stories, DuoRadio, and other formats are available in some courses. The Practice tab offers targeted exercises; some additional options depend on the subscription. Super Duolingo removes advertising and expands practice options, while Max adds AI-supported conversation and explanation features. Availability can vary by language, operating system, and plan, so confirm the current product screen before promising a feature to learners.

The platform is not a substitute for conversation with native speakers or subject-matter editing. Check offline use, device synchronisation, social settings, and classroom controls against the current plan and device configuration before making Duolingo part of a formal programme.

## Rollout, quality control, and operations

Start with five to ten learners, one language, and one bounded outcome. Record the baseline, actual study time, completed exercises, recurring error types, and a short transfer test. Teachers or team leads should review the evidence rather than treating a streak as the result. For children, the pilot also needs appropriate accounts, consent, supervision, and the institution’s safeguarding rules.

Courses evolve continuously, so do not archive a course as if it were an unchanging syllabus. A recurring programme needs an annual content check, fallback material for missing specialist topics, and a clear support route for account or lesson issues.

## Privacy and governance

An account can contain learning progress, exercise responses, device information, and usage data; speaking exercises may add audio or voice data depending on the feature. Social profiles, leaderboards, and reminders are not essential to every learning goal and should be configured deliberately. Before using Duolingo in a school or company, review the current Privacy Policy, retention, processing arrangements, regional data flows, and rules for children.

Do not put sensitive customer information or confidential company examples into free-text fields. A team or school deployment should document account ownership, consent, deletion, visibility, and how learning evidence can be exported or retained. The separate Duolingo English Test is an assessment product; its result should not be equated with an app streak.

## Pricing and total cost

Duolingo uses a freemium model. The free access tier is supported by advertising and feature limits; Super Duolingo and Max are paid subscriptions. Price, billing period, family options, app-store terms, and included Max capabilities may vary by country, platform, and time. Check the plan shown in the account, including renewal and cancellation terms, before purchasing.

Total cost also includes devices, supervision, teaching materials, accessibility work, privacy review, and any tutor or conversation practice. If the organisation needs specialist language or a formal result, a low subscription price can be misleading once assessment and support work are included.

## Editorial Assessment

We recommend Duolingo for self-directed learning, language maintenance, and a low-friction daily routine—especially when a weekly rhythm and a small transfer test are agreed in advance. It creates value when learners practise consistently and can demonstrate progress in a real listening, writing, or speaking task. Choose a specialised course, tutor, or assessment product when exam preparation, professional language, intensive conversation, or a binding competence record is the primary requirement. Our verdict: approve it as a supplementary learning routine, not as the sole proof of language competence.

## FAQ

**Is Duolingo free?**

Yes, a free core tier is available. Advertising, feature access, and limits differ from the paid Super and Max plans; the current offer shown in the account is the reliable reference.

**Is Duolingo enough for professional fluency?**

Not on its own. Add real conversations, job-specific vocabulary, and a test that mirrors the professional situation you care about.

**What should a team measure before rollout?**

Measure starting level, actual study time, error development, and a short transfer task. XP, streaks, and leaderboards should not replace comprehension evidence.

**Which data should we review first?**

Review learning and usage data, optional speaking features, social visibility, deletion, regional processing, and child-safety rules against the current Privacy Policy and your organisation’s requirements.

**When is another tool a better fit?**

Choose an alternative when specialist language, intensive conversation, formal certification, or individual feedback is the main goal. A focused course provider, tutor, or assessment product will usually fit that requirement better.
