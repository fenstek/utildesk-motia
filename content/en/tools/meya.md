# Meya

Meya is a versatile AI platform that helps companies and developers create intelligent chatbots and automation solutions. With a flexible freemium model, Meya offers powerful tools for integrating AI into customer service, marketing, and internal processes. The platform supports both technical and less technical users through a combination of visual editors and programming interfaces.

## Who is Meya for?

Meya is designed for companies of all sizes that want to improve customer communication through the use of AI. The platform is especially suitable for:

- Developers and technical teams who want to build complex chatbots with custom functionality.
- Customer service departments that want to automate repetitive requests and increase efficiency.
- Marketing teams that want to deliver personalized user interactions across different channels.
- Startups and SMEs looking for cost-effective automation solutions.
- Companies that prefer a scalable platform with API support.

## Typical Use Cases

- **Focused rollout:** Meya is a good fit when AI, product, and domain teams want to stop improvising a recurring workflow around chatbot, data.
- **Operations, not demos:** The tool becomes more valuable when prompts, models, outputs, and review steps are documented well enough to survive beyond a one-off trial.
- **Team handovers:** Meya can make responsibilities clearer, so work does not disappear into chats, spreadsheets, or personal accounts.
- **Quality control:** A short review step is especially useful before outputs are published, automated further, or handed over to customers.

## What really matters in daily use

In day-to-day work, Meya is less about having every edge feature and more about whether the team understands where work starts, who reviews it, and how results move forward. A useful setup defines roles, naming rules, and the most important handover points before adoption.

Meya is strongest when it reduces friction in an existing workflow instead of creating a second place to maintain. Before rolling it out widely, test it with real examples: which task becomes faster, which decision becomes clearer, and which manual check should intentionally remain?

## Workflow Fit

Meya fits best into a workflow with a clear input, a traceable work step, and a defined finish line. Small teams can usually keep the process lightweight; larger organizations should also define permissions, approvals, and integrations.

If Meya becomes just another account without ownership, the value fades quickly. Give it a clear place in the existing stack: what enters the tool, what gets decided there, and where the result goes next.

## Privacy & Data

Before adopting Meya, clarify which data will enter the tool and whether model outputs, training data, prompts, and user feedback are involved. The more sensitive the material, the more important permissions, retention rules, export options, and a documented decision on what should stay outside the tool become.

For European teams evaluating Meya, data processing agreements, hosting information, and deletion processes are also worth checking. This is not a substitute for legal advice, but it avoids the common mistake of introducing Meya before the data path is understood.

## Editorial Assessment
