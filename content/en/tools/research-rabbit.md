# Research Rabbit

Research Rabbit is useful when literature search is treated as a network rather than a search box. It shows relationships between papers, authors, and collections, helping users discover relevant sources from a starting point.

Good for students, researchers, and analysts who want to map a field exploratively.

## Who is Research Rabbit for?

Research Rabbit is most useful for teams and individuals that treat a visual research tool as part of a real workflow, not as a novelty. Before adopting it, define the task it should accelerate and where human review still remains necessary.

## Typical use cases

- Discover similar work from core papers
- Understand author and citation networks
- Build literature collections for reviews
- Spot blind spots in a topic area

## Strengths

- Very helpful for exploratory research
- Visualizes relationships instead of only lists
- Good complement to classic databases

## Limits

- Coverage varies by field and source
- Exploration does not replace systematic search strategy
- Findings still need bibliographic checking

## Workflow fit

Research Rabbit makes sense when it has a clear place in the process: intake, production, review, or publishing. Without that role, even a strong tool becomes just another open tab.

## Privacy & data

Collections can reveal research ideas and project focus. For confidential topics, sharing and exports should be used deliberately.

## FAQ

**Is Research Rabbit beginner-friendly?**

It depends on the use case. Simple trials are usually manageable, but production workflows need ownership and quality control.

**When is Research Rabbit worth it?**

When the recurring value is greater than setup, cost, and review effort. For one-off tasks, a lighter tool is often faster.

**What should be checked before adoption?**

Data access, export options, team permissions, pricing model, and whether outputs need review before publishing.
