# MURAL

MURAL is a digital whiteboard for workshops, ideation, mapping, and collaborative planning. It does not replace facilitation, but it creates a shared space where distributed teams can think together visibly.

The difference between good and bad use of MURAL rarely lies in the tool itself. Good boards guide participants through a question; bad boards are sticky-note clouds with the aroma of meetings.

## Who is MURAL suitable for?

MURAL is suitable for product teams, UX, strategy, consulting, education, and remote or hybrid workshops. It is especially helpful when groups need to structure work together, prioritize, or prepare decisions.

## Typical use cases

- Facilitate design-thinking workshops, retrospectives, or ideation sessions.
- Visualize user journeys, service blueprints, or process maps.
- Collect stakeholder input and turn it into clusters.
- Run remote workshops with timers, voting, and clear work phases.
- Make strategy or product questions visible to the group.

## What really matters in day-to-day work

In everyday use, MURAL requires preparation. A good board has a starting point, clear tasks, time boxes, and enough space, but not endless space. Participants should not have to learn how to think and click during the workshop itself.

After the workshop, cleanup is mandatory: decisions, open points, and next steps need to be pulled out of the board. Otherwise, the nice canvas remains a digital campfire with no consequences.

## Key features

- Digital whiteboards with sticky notes, shapes, images, and connectors.
- Templates for workshops, planning, and analysis.
- Voting, timers, facilitation features, and presentation modes.
- Real-time collaboration for distributed teams.
- Export and integration options for documentation and follow-up work.

## Pros and limitations

### Pros

- Very good for visual thinking in remote or hybrid groups.
- Many workshop features are designed directly for facilitation.
- Templates speed up recurring workshop formats.

### Limitations

- Without facilitation, the board quickly becomes cluttered.
- Very large boards can become cognitively and technically sluggish.
- Decisions must be transferred into operational tools after the workshop.

## Workflow fit

MURAL fits workshop processes with preparation, live facilitation, and follow-up. It should not be mistaken for a project management system, but rather as a space for thinking and decision-making from which concrete tasks emerge.

A good MURAL workshop does not end with a full board, but with three to five clear outcomes. Anyone who exports tasks, decisions, and open questions at the end prevents collaborative energy from evaporating.

## Privacy & data

Boards may contain strategy, customer data, or internal conflicts. Access permissions, guest links, and exports should be set deliberately. After sensitive workshops, it is worth collecting sharing permissions again.

## Editorial assessment

MURAL is strong when workshops are truly guided. The tool provides the surface; quality comes from good questions, clear time boxes, and consistent follow-up.

A good first test for MURAL is therefore not a demo click, but a real mini workflow: facilitate design-thinking workshops, retrospectives, or ideation sessions. If that works with real data, real roles, and a clear outcome, the next expansion stage is worth it.

At the same time, the most important limitation should be stated openly: without facilitation, the board quickly becomes cluttered. This friction is not a reason to rule it out, but it belongs in the decision before purchase, not only in the frustrated debrief afterward.

## FAQ

**Is MURAL suitable for small teams?**

**What should a MURAL pilot look like?**

Start with a bounded process, a small group and a clear success criterion. Check output quality, permissions and handovers before expanding the scope.

**Which data should not be processed in MURAL without review?**

Sensitive or confidential content should wait until contract terms, access, storage and deletion controls have been reviewed. Escalate uncertainty to the responsible privacy owner.

**When is an alternative to MURAL the better choice?**
