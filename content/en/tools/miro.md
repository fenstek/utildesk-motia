# Miro

Miro is a versatile online whiteboard platform that helps teams collaborate, visualize ideas, and plan projects efficiently. With a wide range of tools and integrations, Miro is especially well suited for design processes, brainstorming sessions, and workflow automation. Its combination of AI-powered features and an intuitive interface makes Miro an important tool for productivity and creative collaboration.

## Who is Miro suitable for?

Miro is designed for teams and individuals across a variety of industries who value visual collaboration. It is especially suitable for:

- Creative and design teams developing concepts and prototypes
- Project managers who plan and coordinate workflows
- Teams in product development and marketing that run interactive workshops
- Educational institutions for interactive learning and presentation formats
- Companies that want to automate processes and increase productivity

The platform is scalable for both small teams and large organizations and offers features that adapt to different requirements.

## Key Features

- **Unlimited whiteboards:** Create and organize as many digital whiteboards as you need for different projects.
- **Template library:** Access a large selection of ready-made templates for brainstorming, Agile methods, UX/UI design, and more.
- **Real-time collaboration:** Multiple users can work on a board at the same time, add comments, and make changes.
- **AI-powered tools:** Automated suggestions, text generation, and visual aids to support creative processes.
- **Integrations:** Seamless connections to tools such as Slack, Jira, Trello, Google Drive, and more.
- **Interactive widgets:** Add charts, mind maps, sticky notes, timers, and other elements for structure.
- **Presentation mode:** Easily share and present content directly from the platform.
- **Access and permissions management:** Control who can view or edit boards.
- **Offline access:** The ability to edit boards offline and sync them later.
- **Mobile apps:** Access and edit boards on iOS and Android devices.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- Intuitive and user-friendly interface
- Extensive real-time collaboration capabilities
- Wide range of templates and AI-powered features
- Flexible integration with other work tools
- Scalable for teams of any size
- Boosts productivity through automation and visualization

### Cons

- Some advanced features are only available in paid plans
- The wide range of features can feel overwhelming for new users at first
- Offline functionality is limited compared with online use
- Performance may be affected on very large boards or with many users

## Pricing & Costs

Miro offers several pricing plans that vary depending on features and team size:

- **Free version:** Basic features, limited number of boards and team members.
- **Team plan:** Advanced collaboration and management features, usually billed monthly or annually.
- **Business plan:** Additional security and integration options, ideal for larger teams.
- **Enterprise plan:** Customized solutions with advanced administration and support features.

Exact prices depend on the selected plan and the number of users and can be found on the official website.

## Miro Alternatives

- **Lucidspark:** Another collaborative whiteboard focused on brainstorming and project planning.
- **Microsoft Whiteboard:** Seamless integration into the Microsoft ecosystem, ideal for Office 365 users.
- **Conceptboard:** Particularly well suited for visual collaboration and feedback processes.
- **Jamboard:** Google’s whiteboard solution with simple operation and integration into Google Workspace.
- **Stormboard:** A collaboration platform focused on idea management and Agile methods.

## FAQ

**1. Is Miro free for individuals?**  
Yes, Miro offers a free version with limited features that is well suited for individuals or small teams.

**2. What AI features does Miro offer?**  
Miro integrates AI-based tools for automatic text creation, suggestions for visualizations, and improved workflow automation.

**3. Can I integrate Miro with other applications?**  
Yes, Miro supports numerous integrations, including Slack, Jira, Trello, Google Drive, and many more.

**4. How secure is my data with Miro?**  
Miro follows modern security standards and offers advanced security features such as single sign-on and compliance options in higher-tier plans.

**5. Is there a mobile app for Miro?**  
Yes, Miro is available as an app for iOS and Android, so you can work on boards while on the go.

**6. Can I use Miro offline?**  
Some features are available offline, but full functionality and synchronization depend on an internet connection.

**7. How many users can work on a board at the same time?**  
Miro supports real-time collaboration for multiple users, and the exact number may vary depending on the plan.

**8. How do I get started with Miro?**  
You can sign up for free and start with a board right away. Templates and tutorials help you get started.
