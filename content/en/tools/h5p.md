# H5P

H5P is an open-source framework for creating interactive learning content. It is used to build quizzes, interactive videos, course presentations, timelines, drag-and-drop exercises, flashcards, and other reusable learning modules.

## Who is H5P for?

H5P is useful for educators, instructional designers, universities, schools, training teams, and developers who need interactive educational content that can be embedded into learning platforms and websites.

## Key features

- Many ready-made interactive content types.
- Browser-based authoring workflow.
- Embedding into learning platforms and websites.
- Open-source foundation and broad education community.
- Useful for quizzes, videos, exercises, presentations, and self-checks.
- Integration with platforms such as Moodle, WordPress, and Drupal.

## Pros and cons

### Pros

- Open source and widely used in education.
- Fast way to add interactivity to learning materials.
- Large library of content types.
- Good fit for LMS and website integration.

### Cons

- Visual design can feel generic without customization.
- Complex learning experiences may need additional instructional design work.
- Hosting, plugins, and platform compatibility must be managed.

## Pricing and costs

H5P is open source. Depending on the setup, costs may come from hosting, LMS integration, maintenance, or commercial H5P services.

## Alternatives to H5P

- **Articulate Storyline:** Professional e-learning authoring software.
- **Adobe Captivate:** Course authoring and simulation tool.
- **iSpring Suite:** PowerPoint-based e-learning authoring.
- **Moodle activities:** Native interactive activities in Moodle.
- **Genially:** Browser-based interactive content creation.

## FAQ

**Is H5P free?**
The core technology is open source. Some hosted or commercial services may require payment.

**Can H5P be used in Moodle?**
Yes. H5P is commonly used in Moodle and other learning platforms.

**Is H5P only for quizzes?**
No. Quizzes are common, but H5P supports many interactive content types.

## Editorial assessment

H5P should not be judged by its feature list alone. The useful question is whether it improves a real workflow for development, testing, infrastructure or technical handover without creating new coordination or control gaps.

A good evaluation starts small: one real use case, one responsible owner, clear input data and a result that can be reviewed after one or two weeks. Only then does it become clear whether H5P actually improves the process or simply adds another interface to daily work.

- **Good starting point:** Test H5P on a limited workflow before turning it into a general default.
- **Review point:** Before rollout, define how repository rules, review, tests, permissions and rollback will be documented and checked.
- **Main limit:** Without ownership, data hygiene or review, H5P can look more useful in a demo than it becomes in production.
