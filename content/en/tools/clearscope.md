# Clearscope

Clearscope is a platform for SEO and content teams. It studies a search query and the pages ranking for it, surfaces topic gaps, and supports the workflow from brief to revision. Its core value is not automatic text generation; it is a shared workspace for search intent, topical coverage, and editorial decisions.

## Who is Clearscope for?

Clearscope fits content teams, in-house SEOs, and agencies that repeat the same cycle: choose a topic, build a brief, write, review, and refresh. A subscription is harder to justify for one occasional blog post. Basic SEO knowledge still matters because a score cannot replace research, subject expertise, or editorial accountability.

## Typical use cases

- **Plan a new article:** Research a query, collect relevant terms and questions, and turn them into a useful brief and outline.
- **Refresh an existing page:** Compare an older guide with current search results and decide which gaps are genuinely useful instead of inserting terms mechanically.
- **Standardize agency delivery:** Share briefs and reports with writers, clients, and approvers so the reasoning behind changes stays visible.
- **Maintain a content library:** Use Content Inventory and, where appropriate, a connected Google Search Console property to prioritize audits and refreshes.

## How the workflow works

Clearscope starts with a target query and creates a Content Draft. It analyzes relevant search results and presents suggested terms, audience questions, competitor outlines, and citable sources. In the Editor, Content Grade, readability, and term coverage update while the team writes or revises.

The output is best treated as decision support. Clearscope shows what appears frequently in the analyzed results; it does not decide what is accurate, original, on-brand, or useful for a particular audience.

## Main features

- **Content Drafts:** SERP-based analysis with terms, questions, competitor structures, and source suggestions.
- **Editor and Content Grade:** Real-time feedback on topical coverage, term usage, word count, and readability.
- **Topic Explorations:** Research related topics, keyword variations, search intent, and possible content gaps.
- **AI and search visibility tracking:** Monitor selected topics and visibility across search engines and AI answers where the plan includes those capabilities.
- **Content Inventory:** Organize published pages and prioritize updates; connecting Google Search Console adds a more useful performance view.
- **Sharing, export, and writing integrations:** Clearscope documents integrations or workflows for Google Docs, WordPress, and Microsoft Word, subject to the current plan and product setup.

## Limits and risks

- A high Content Grade is not a ranking guarantee. Links, technical SEO, brand strength, originality, and answer quality are outside that single metric.
- SERP recommendations are a snapshot for a chosen query, language, region, and sometimes local target. Treat them carefully when results are personalized or changing quickly.
- Competitor terms should never become keyword stuffing. Editorial relevance, source checking, and a clear answer matter more than maximizing a score.
- Clearscope is subscription- and credit-based. The official plans use limits for tracked prompts, pages, Topic Explorations, and drafts; extra capacity may cost more. Compare expected monthly volume with the current plan before buying.
- Do not send sensitive customer data, unpublished material, or personal information into an external analysis or AI service without checking permissions, retention, and processing terms.

## Editorial Assessment

Clearscope is a strong fit when a team wants to discuss content quality against explicit search intent and make refresh work more repeatable. Its practical value is a shared brief and review checkpoint, not automatically generated prose. I would start with one real content cluster and measure whether briefing, review, and maintenance become faster or more consistent.

It is a poor fit if you only need occasional free keyword ideas, have little content to maintain, or expect a promise of organic rankings. In those cases, a lean research workflow, Search Console data, and sound editorial judgment may be enough.

## Pricing and planning

Clearscope sells subscriptions with different allowances and feature sets. Its current pricing page lists Essentials, Business, and Enterprise; the important constraint is not only the number of users but also credits for prompts, pages, Topic Explorations, and drafts. Enterprise adds custom allowances, agreements, and SSO. Prices and limits can change, so check the official pricing page before committing.

## Alternatives

- [Surfer SEO](/en/tools/surfer-seo/): better suited when content optimization needs to sit alongside broader SERP, audit, and keyword workflows.
- [MarketMuse](/en/tools/marketmuse/): a stronger candidate for topic coverage, content planning, and larger content libraries.
- [SEMrush](/en/tools/semrush/): the broader choice when content is one part of an SEO, backlink, competitor, and PPC suite.
- [Frase](/en/tools/frase/): a pragmatic option for briefs, question research, and content optimization with a stronger writing-workflow emphasis.

## FAQ

**1. Does Clearscope replace an SEO editor?**
No. It structures search data and gives recommendations. People remain responsible for accuracy, sources, voice, and publication.

**2. What does Content Grade mean?**
It is a real-time signal for topical coverage and relevance compared with the analyzed competition. It is not a full-page quality score or a Google ranking guarantee.

**3. Can I review existing content?**
Yes. Existing drafts can be revised in the Editor, while Content Inventory is intended for managing larger collections. Prioritize updates using real performance and business goals as well.

**4. Which languages and regions are supported?**
Drafts can be configured for a suitable language and search location. The official documentation lists English, German, French, Spanish, and Italian among the supported languages. Coverage and result quality can vary by market and query.

**5. Do I need Google Search Console?**
Not for every Draft. It is mainly useful for performance views and importing your own pages into Content Inventory.

**6. Is Clearscope suitable for small teams?**
Yes, if the team produces enough content to use the subscription allowance. For only a few articles per year, a project-based or lighter tool stack is usually more economical.
